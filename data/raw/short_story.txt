The old lighthouse had been dark for twenty years. Every fisherman in town knew to avoid the rocks near Pelican Point after sunset, and most did.
Marcus didn't.
He'd grown up hearing the stories but never believed them. Ghosts, cursed waters, a keeper who never left — all nonsense, he figured. Just old people scaring children away from dangerous cliffs.
The night his boat engine died, he finally understood why they believed.
The light came on.
Nobody was up there. He knew that for a fact. But the beam swept across the water anyway, slow and steady, guiding him safely around the rocks and into the harbor.
He never fished after dark again. And he never told anyone what he saw. Some stories, he decided, were better left to the old people.
